A Reflection on McLuhan, M. ‘The Medium is the Message’

Today I read a paper called The Medium is the Message by Marshall McLuhan. Though the piece is representative of its time in a few ways, such as treatment of Africans as universally illiterate, it is also foresightful in its conception of “electric media” as a force of significant societal change, and thoughtful in considering the connection inherent between the medium of transmission and the message within. After reading, I was aware of a significant prediction in the piece. McLuhan mentions that “each of the media is also a powerful weapon with which to clobber other media and other groups. The result is that the present age has been one of multiple civil wars that are not limited to the world of art and entertainment” (McLuhan, 1964).

This has proven true in every new form of media we have created since McLuhan wrote this paper, perhaps even progressively so, with the newest forms of media, social media, featuring extraordinary amounts of direct conflict (Anderson, 2018) (Leighton, 2019). It is also the case that this trend of increasing conflict follows the trend of increasing interactability of new media. If this understanding is correct, then over time our conflicts in the real world may continue to be superseded by conflicts in the progressively interactable realm of digital media. It may be the case then, that as these new media become the “technological staples”, the “unique cultural flavor” (McLuhan, 1964) they bring may be one of division and discord.
